The Hazy Forest

"The strangest, saddest town I believe I have ever seen‚Äù
- Bill Bryson, A Walk in the Woods, 1998.


Dense forest grows denser as nature overtakes what remains of Centralia. Old roads wind through the overgrown forest and end abruptly, in smoking chasms that are scarred into the asphalt. Sinkholes open beneath neighborhoods and swallow the city piece by piece, creating a post-urban maze of decay and rubble.

Centralia Pennsylvania is among the few lesser-known geographical oddities of northeast America. Once a prosperous coal mining town, the city was built atop a sprawling labyrinth of coal seams that caught fire in 1962. A smokey haze blankets the undergrowth as the toxic gases of the coal mine rise through the topsoil from the blaze burning just beneath the surface.

Evidence of past efforts to save the town can be seen. Rusting, aged ventilation pipes jut out of the scorched landscape and feed the toxic fog, the last remnants of the desperate attempt to lessen the heat and pressure of the hell below. Old signs posted on tree trunks and fenceposts warn passersby of the dangers they may face.

Some visitors may feel an uncanny sense of familiarity towards Centralia. This may be due to the fact that it was the inspiration behind several settings in popular literature and television. Specifically, the film adaptation of the horror game headliner, Silent Hill. It is also described in vivid detail as the settings for both Strange Highways by thriller writer Dean Koontz, and Vampire Zero by horror writer David Wellington.
